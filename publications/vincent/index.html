The unusual Gasteromycetes Lycogalopsis solmsii belongs to the gomphoid-phalloid group.

VINCENT DEMOULIN, LUC CORNET, EMILIE DELBRUYÈRE and DENIS BAURAIN The rare tropical Gasteromycetes Lycogalopsis solmsii has been found twice at thirty years interval in the Singapore Botanic Gardens. From the most recent find a culture could be isolated, which allowed DNA extraction and sequencing of about 2000 bp from the nuclear ribosomal DNA. Comparison to a large sample of Basidiomycetes was only possible for a part of the large ribosomal subunit, but clearly indicated affiliation to the gomphoid-phalloid group, without any relationship to Lycoperdales or Agaricales, as stated in the Dictionary of the Fungi.
